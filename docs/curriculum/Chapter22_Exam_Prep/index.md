# Phase 9 — Exam Preparation

> **Goal:** pass any of the 5 GitHub certifications on first attempt — Foundations, Copilot, Actions, GHAS, Admin.

Companion to Chapter 9 in [BOOK.md](../index.md), which contains 5 mock exams of 30 questions each (**150 questions total**) with answers and explanations.

## The 5 exams

| Code | Title | Length | Pass mark | Typical prep |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| GH-900 | **GitHub Foundations** | 75 min, 75 Q | ~70% | 1 week |
| GH-300 | **GitHub Copilot** | 65 min, 60 Q | ~70% | 1 week |
| GH-200 | **GitHub Actions** | 75 min, 60 Q | ~70% | 2 weeks |
| GH-500 | **GitHub Advanced Security (GHAS)** | 75 min, 60 Q | ~70% | 2 weeks |
| GH-100 | **GitHub Administration** | 75 min, 75 Q | ~70% | 2 weeks |

## Study plan (4 weeks per exam)

- Week 1 — read the corresponding chapter end-to-end.
- Week 2 — re-read; do all drills; write your own one-page cheat sheet.
- Week 3 — take Mock A or B (from Chapter 9). Score yourself honestly.
- Week 4 — review every missed question; retake; **schedule the exam**.

## Scoring rubric

After taking a mock:

- **≥ 80%** — schedule the exam this week.
- **70–79%** — re-do exercises in weak chapters; retake mock; book exam.
- **< 70%** — re-read the chapter; revisit the capstone in Phase 10.

## Day-of-exam checklist

- Stable internet, quiet room.
- Webcam, government ID.
- No second screen.
- Bottle of water.
- Run a hardware test 24 h before.
- Don't read forums in the last 24 h — review only your own cheat sheet.
